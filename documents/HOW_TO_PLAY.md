# How to Play Wahoo

## Goal

Be the first player to get all 4 of your marbles into your home row.

## Setup

- 4 players, 4 marbles each, all starting in your base.
- One six-sided die.
- Highest roll goes first; play passes clockwise.

## Your Turn

Roll the die and move one marble according to the rules below. **If you can make a legal move, you must.** If you have no legal move, your turn passes.

**Rolling a 6 lets you roll again.** No limit on consecutive 6s.

## Moving Your Marbles

**Getting out of base.** Roll a 1 or a 6 to move a marble from your base onto your starting square on the track. The marble lands there regardless of which number you rolled.

**On the track.** Move forward exactly the number of squares you rolled. You can't land on or pass over your own marbles. Landing on an opponent's marble sends it back to their base (you can pass over opponents without effect).

**Into home.** Once a marble reaches its home-entry trigger square, it must turn into your home row on its next move. You don't need an exact roll to turn in, but you do need an exact roll to land in any home slot — you can't overshoot. If you can't make a legal home move, that marble can't move at all that turn.

**Inside home.** Marbles in your home row are safe and can't be captured. You can keep advancing them deeper with exact rolls.

## The Center Shortcut

The center hole is a major shortcut. To take it:

- Your marble must be within the first 6 squares after leaving base.
- Roll the exact number that would land you on the 6th square. From square 1, that's a 6. From square 2, a 5. From square 3, a 4. And so on down to square 6, which takes a 1.
- Taking the shortcut is **optional** — you can always choose to advance normally instead.

To exit the center, roll a 1. Your marble lands close to home, skipping most of the loop.

**The catch:** while your marble sits in the center, any opponent who rolls the right number to enter the center will bump you back to base. The center holds only one marble at a time.

**One shot per marble.** Once a marble moves past the entry window, it can't take the shortcut later in that trip.

## Winning

You win the moment your fourth marble enters your home row. The slots don't have to be filled in any particular order — every marble just needs to be somewhere in home.
